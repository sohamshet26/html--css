Types

Melenoma

What is it: Melanoma is a type of skin cancer that develops in the cells (melanocytes) that produce melanin, the pigment that gives your skin its color. It can also form in your eyes and, rarely, in internal organs, such as your intestines.

How does it happen: Melanoma occurs when something goes wrong in the melanin-producing cells (melanocytes) that give color to your skin. Normally, skin cells develop in a controlled and orderly way â€” healthy new cells push older cells toward your skin's surface, where they die and eventually fall off. But when some cells develop DNA damage, new cells may begin to grow out of control and can eventually form a mass of cancerous cells.

Squamous Cell Carcinoma

What is it:Squamous cell carcinoma (SCC) is a common form of skin cancer that develops in the squamous cells that make up the outer layer of the skin.

How does it happen:SCC is mainly caused by prolonged exposure to ultraviolet (UV) radiation from the sun or from tanning beds. This exposure can damage the DNA in the squamous cells, leading to uncontrolled growth and cancer.

Treatment: Like BCC, SCC is also treatable if detected early. Treatment options include surgical excision, radiation therapy, and other treatments depending on the size, location, and depth of the tumor.

Basal cell carcinoma

What is it:Basal cell carcinoma (BCC) is a type of skin cancer that begins in the basal cells, which are responsible for producing new skin cells as old ones die off.

How does it happen: BCC is also mainly caused by UV radiation from the sun or tanning beds. The damage from UV radiation can lead to mutations in the basal cells, causing them to grow out of control and form a cancerous tumor.

Treatment: Treatable and usually not life-threatening if detected and treated early. Treatment options include surgical removal, topical medications, and other therapies.
